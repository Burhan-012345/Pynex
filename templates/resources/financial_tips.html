Financial Tips

Expert advice to help you make smarter financial decisions

The 50/30/20 Rule

Allocate 50% of income to needs, 30% to wants, and 20% to savings. This simple framework helps maintain financial balance.

Track Every Expense

Record all purchases, no matter how small. You'll be surprised how those little expenses add up over time.

Review Weekly

Set aside 15 minutes each week to review your spending. Regular check-ins prevent small issues from becoming big problems.

Plan for Irregular Expenses

Set aside money each month for annual subscriptions, car maintenance, or holiday gifts to avoid budget surprises.

Reduce Dining Out

Cutting back on restaurant meals by just 2-3 times per month can save hundreds of dollars annually.

Automate Savings

Set up automatic transfers to savings accounts. If you don't see the money, you're less likely to spend it.

Advanced Financial Strategies

Emergency Fund Basics

Every adult should have 3-6 months of living expenses saved in an emergency fund. Start small - even ₹500-1000 per month adds up quickly.

Debt Snowball Method

List debts from smallest to largest. Pay minimums on all, but put extra money toward the smallest debt first. The psychological wins keep you motivated.

Smart Grocery Shopping

  • Always shop with a list
  • Never shop hungry
  • Buy generic brands for staple items
  • Use cashback and reward apps
  • Plan meals around weekly sales

Subscription Audit

Review all your subscriptions monthly. Cancel any you haven't used in the past 30 days. The average person spends ₹2000+ annually on unused subscriptions.

Pro Tip: The 24-Hour Rule

For any non-essential purchase over ₹2000, wait 24 hours before buying. This cooling-off period prevents impulse purchases you might regret.
